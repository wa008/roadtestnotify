You're refreshing the DriveTest booking page, hoping a slot opens up before your appointment three months from now. But you're checking at 10 AM on a Tuesday, and almost nothing is appearing. That's because road test cancellations don't release evenly throughout the day. There are specific hours when slots flood the system, and specific hours when checking is a waste of your time.

We tracked cancellation releases across Ontario DriveTest centres and logged when each slot appeared. The data covers 1,436 individual cancellation events. Here's what the hourly breakdown reveals.

## The Evening Window: 5 PM to 8 PM

The single most productive time to check for cancellations is between 5 PM and 8 PM. During these four hours, 567 out of 1,436 total cancellations appeared. That's nearly 40% of all released slots, concentrated in one-sixth of the day.

The peak hits at 6 PM and 7 PM, with 167 cancellations logged at each hour. At 8 PM the count is still strong at 118, before dropping sharply to just 4 at 9 PM. The 5 PM hour contributes 115 cancellations of its own, making every hour in this window a productive one.

Why the evening surge? The most likely explanation is batch processing. DriveTest's booking system appears to release cancelled slots in waves rather than instantly, and the largest wave hits in the early evening. People who cancel during business hours may not see their slot reappear until the system runs its next release cycle. The 5 PM to 8 PM window catches the biggest batch of the day.

If you can only check once per day, check between 6 PM and 7 PM. That two-hour window alone accounts for 334 cancellations, over 23% of the daily total.

## The Early Morning Spike: 6 AM to 7 AM

The second-best window is first thing in the morning. At 6 AM, 96 cancellations appeared. At 7 AM, the count rises to 110, the third-highest single hour of the day after the twin evening peaks at 6 PM and 7 PM.

By 8 AM the number drops to 45, and by 9 AM it falls to just 31, the lowest count during waking hours. The early morning spike likely represents an overnight batch: cancellations made the previous evening and overnight that get released when the system processes them at the start of the new day.

This makes the 6 AM to 7 AM window especially useful if you're an early riser. You're checking before most people are awake, and the supply of new slots is relatively high. The combination of fresh inventory and low competition is hard to beat.

## Midday and Afternoon: Uneven but Worth Watching

The middle of the day isn't dead, but it's less predictable. After the morning drop-off, cancellations climb back to 59 at 10 AM and 61 at 11 AM. A notable bump at noon brings 105 cancellations, one of the stronger hours outside the evening and early-morning windows.

The afternoon is uneven. 1 PM drops to 58, then 2 PM spikes to 117, ranking among the top five hours of the day. 3 PM pulls back to 74, then 4 PM recovers to 107 before the evening surge takes over at 5 PM.

The 2 PM spike is worth highlighting. At 117 cancellations, it nearly matches the evening peak hours and sits right in the middle of the workday. If you have a desk job and can spare two minutes to refresh the booking page at 2 PM, the data says that's a productive moment to do it.

The full midday-to-afternoon block (10 AM to 4 PM) produces about 581 cancellations across seven hours. That's roughly 40% of the daily total, but at less than half the per-hour density of the evening window. If you're checking throughout the day, these hours will produce results, just not as consistently as the evening or early morning.

## When Not to Bother

Between midnight and 5 AM, the data shows zero cancellations. Not low. Zero. The system does not release slots during these hours. Checking the DriveTest website in the middle of the night will not produce results.

After 9 PM the numbers become negligible: 4 at 9 PM, 1 at 10 PM, 1 at 11 PM. By that point the day's release cycles are finished. Save your evening checking for before 8 PM.

The weakest period during waking hours is 8 AM to 9 AM, with only 45 and 31 cancellations respectively. This is counterintuitive. You might expect the start of business hours to bring fresh slots. Instead, the system appears to have already released its morning batch at 6 AM and 7 AM, and the next meaningful wave doesn't build until mid-morning.

**Time your manual checks.** If you're refreshing the DriveTest page yourself, concentrate your effort in the 6 PM to 7 PM window and the 6 AM to 7 AM window. Checking five times during those hours is more productive than checking twenty times spread throughout the day.
