# Northern Ontario vs GTA DriveTest Pass Rates

If you have been browsing [pass rates across Ontario DriveTest centres](/pass-rates/), one pattern jumps out: northern centres consistently outperform GTA centres by a wide margin. The average G2 pass rate across the 12 Northern Ontario locations is 78%. At the 11 GTA locations, it is 64%. That is a 14-point gap, and it holds for the G test too. This article breaks down the numbers region by region, explains what is behind the difference, and helps you decide whether booking outside the GTA makes sense.

## The Regional Averages

We calculated the mean pass rate for every region using data from all 56 Ontario DriveTest centres. Here is how they stack up for the G2 test:

- **Northern Ontario** (12 centres): 78%
- **Central Ontario** (8 centres): 75%
- **Eastern Ontario** (11 centres): 75%
- **Southwestern Ontario** (14 centres): 71%
- **GTA** (11 centres): 64%

The G test follows the same pattern:

- **Northern Ontario**: 77%
- **Central Ontario**: 75%
- **Eastern Ontario**: 73%
- **Southwestern Ontario**: 70%
- **GTA**: 63%

The gap between Northern Ontario and the GTA is 14 points for both tests. Even Southwestern Ontario, which includes mid-sized cities like London (77% G2) and Kitchener (64% G2), sits about 7 points above the GTA average. The trend is clear and consistent: the farther you get from the GTA, the higher the average pass rate climbs. Central and Eastern Ontario occupy a middle band, roughly splitting the difference between the northern and southern extremes.

## Why Northern Centres Outperform the GTA

Three factors explain most of the difference.

**Road complexity.** GTA test routes run through multi-lane intersections, dense pedestrian zones, and heavy traffic. A left turn at a six-lane intersection in Brampton demands more precise gap judgment and mirror checks than a left turn on a quiet two-lane road in Espanola. The scoring criteria are standardized province-wide, so every examiner uses the same checklist regardless of location. But the environment changes how easy it is to demonstrate each skill without picking up errors. More traffic means more chances for something to go wrong, even for a skilled driver.

**Test volume and conditions.** High-volume centres like Brampton and Toronto Downsview process thousands of tests per year. Northern centres like Kapuskasing and Kenora handle far fewer. Lower volume tends to mean calmer parking lots, less congestion on surrounding roads during test hours, and a generally less hectic atmosphere. When you are not navigating past three other test vehicles and a lineup of nervous drivers in the parking area, the experience starts on a different note.

**Driver demographics.** The GTA has a higher proportion of newcomers to Canada who are adapting to Ontario road rules and driving culture at the same time they are preparing for the test. Northern centres tend to serve drivers who grew up on local roads, many of whom started driving on rural or gravel routes in their teens. By the time these drivers sit for their G2 test, they often have significantly more hours behind the wheel. That familiarity translates directly into test performance.

None of this means GTA examiners score more harshly. The test is the same test everywhere. The conditions on the road are what differ, and they affect outcomes at scale.

## Should You Travel North for a Higher Pass Rate?

On paper, booking at Kapuskasing instead of Brampton gives you a 30-point statistical advantage. But the decision is more complicated than it looks.

The biggest issue is familiarity. The pass rate at each centre reflects people who live and drive in that area every day. A Brampton driver who books in North Bay loses the single biggest advantage that local drivers have: they know the roads. You will not know which intersections have awkward signal timing, where the school zones are, or which turns the examiner tends to include. Driving in unfamiliar territory adds mental load on a day when you want to be relaxed and automatic in your habits.

A more realistic approach is to look at centres within a two- to three-hour drive of Toronto that still sit well above the GTA average. Barrie has a 70% G2 pass rate and 72% for the G test. Belleville comes in at 79% G2 and 78% G. Orillia posts 77% for the G2 and 74% for the G test. If you can visit one of these towns the weekend before your test to practise the local roads, you combine a statistical edge with the familiarity advantage that matters on test day.

Availability is the other variable that often matters more than pass rates.
