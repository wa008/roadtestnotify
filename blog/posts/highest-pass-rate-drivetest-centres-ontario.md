Not all Ontario DriveTest centres produce the same pass rates. The gap between the highest and lowest G2 pass rates across the province is a full 30 percentage points. Whether that spread comes down to route difficulty, traffic conditions, or the mix of test-takers at each location, the numbers are worth knowing before you pick where to book. We used pass rate data from all 56 Ontario DriveTest centres to build a complete ranking. Here is what stands out.

## The 10 Highest G2 Pass Rate Centres

Kapuskasing leads Ontario with an 88% G2 pass rate and an 84% G pass rate. The rest of the top 10 follows a clear pattern: Northern Ontario and smaller Central Ontario towns occupy nearly every spot.

1. Kapuskasing (Northern Ontario) — 88% G2, 84% G
2. Espanola (Northern Ontario) — 86% G2, 81% G
3. Kenora (Northern Ontario) — 84% G2, 82% G
4. Bancroft (Central Ontario) — 83% G2, 86% G
5. North Bay (Northern Ontario) — 83% G2, 80% G
6. Owen Sound (Central Ontario) — 83% G2, 81% G
7. Timmins (Northern Ontario) — 83% G2, 79% G
8. Huntsville (Central Ontario) — 82% G2, 77% G
9. Winchester (Eastern Ontario) — 81% G2, 80% G
10. Belleville (Eastern Ontario) — 79% G2, 78% G

Five of these centres are in Northern Ontario. The remaining five split between Central and Eastern Ontario. Not a single GTA location appears anywhere near this list.

Bancroft deserves a closer look. Its G pass rate of 86% is the highest in the entire province for the full G road test, beating even Kapuskasing's 84%. If you already hold your G2 and are preparing for the G exam, Bancroft is statistically the strongest-performing centre in Ontario.

The bottom of the ranking tells the opposite story. GTA centres dominate the list.

1. Brampton (GTA) — 58% G2, 58% G

3. Orangeville (Central Ontario) — 60% G2, 62% G
4. Toronto Etobicoke (GTA) — 61% G2, 59% G
5. Newmarket (GTA) — 61% G2, 63% G
6. Mississauga (GTA) — 63% G2, 64% G
7. Brantford (Southwestern Ontario) — 63% G2, 67% G
8. Toronto Metro East (GTA) — 63% G2, 63% G

Tillsonburg and Woodstock also sit at 64% G2, just outside the bottom 10.

Brampton and Toronto Downsview share the lowest G pass rate in Ontario at 58%. Toronto Etobicoke follows closely at 59%. Six of the 10 lowest-ranked centres for G2 are inside the GTA. Thunder Bay is the only Northern Ontario centre in the bottom 10, making it a clear outlier for the region.

## What Explains the 30-Point Gap

The 30-point spread between Kapuskasing's 88% and Brampton's 58% is too large to write off as noise. Several factors likely feed into it.

Traffic and road complexity play a significant role. GTA test routes pass through dense intersections, multi-lane roads, and heavy pedestrian traffic. A wrong-lane merge in Brampton during rush hour is a very different experience from a left turn on a quiet side street in Kapuskasing. More variables on the road during your test mean more chances to pick up deductible errors.

Test volume matters too. High-volume centres like Brampton and Mississauga process far more road tests per year than a centre like Espanola or Bancroft. Higher-volume centres tend to attract a wider cross-section of preparedness levels, which can pull the average pass rate down. The scheduling pressure at busy centres may also affect the testing atmosphere for both candidates and examiners.

Demographics are another factor. GTA centres serve a large share of newer Ontario residents and first-time licence holders who may have less accumulated experience on Ontario roads specifically. Northern and rural centres tend to draw candidates who have been driving locally on their G1 for months, giving them a strong familiarity with the area and road conditions before they ever sit the test.

The scoring criteria themselves are standardized province-wide. An examiner in Brampton uses the same checklist and the same point system as one in Owen Sound. But the road environment in which you are scored differs dramatically between a dense urban centre and a quiet town, and those differences show up in the pass rate data. ## G2 vs G Pass Rates: Do They Move Together?

At most centres, the G2 and G pass rates land within two or three points of each other. But there are exceptions worth noting.

Bancroft posts a G pass rate of 86% against a G2 rate of 83%, a 3-point gap in favour of the G test. This makes intuitive sense. G test-takers already hold a G2 and typically bring years of on-road experience. At a small, low-volume centre like Bancroft, that additional experience shows up clearly in the numbers.

At the other end, Toronto Port Union has a 66% G2 pass rate but drops to 62% on the G test. The 4-point decline likely reflects the highway driving component that the G test adds. Port Union sits near Highway 401, and merging into heavy expressway traffic is a challenge that a G2 road test does not include. Centres near major highways may see a steeper drop between G2 and G results for this reason.

Across all 56 centres, the G pass rate is slightly lower than the G2 rate on average. The highway section of the G test adds a layer of difficulty, even in smaller towns where highway traffic is lighter.

## How to Use This Data

If you have flexibility on where you test, these numbers give you something concrete to weigh. A centre with an 83% pass rate does not guarantee you will pass, and a centre at 58% does not mean you will fail. But testing in a less congested area may reduce the number of high-stress variables you face during the exam.

There is an important caveat. You should always practice on the roads near the centre where you plan to test. Driving three hours north to a high-pass-rate location without knowing local intersections or traffic patterns is not a reliable strategy. Familiarity with the test area often matters more than a few percentage points on a provincial chart.
