Not every DriveTest centre in Ontario produces the same results. Pass rates range from 58% to 88% depending on where you test — a gap of 30 percentage points that can shape whether you walk out with your licence or end up booking a retest. If you have flexibility in where you take your road test, this data can help you make a smarter choice.

We pulled the numbers from all 57 DriveTest centres across the province and ranked them by both G and G2 results. The trends are striking, and the top-performing centres share some clear patterns worth understanding before you book.

## Top 10 centres by G pass rate

The G road test is the final step to a full Ontario licence. It includes a highway driving component and tests more advanced skills than the G2. Across the province, the G test shows wide variation in outcomes, with the highest-scoring centres clustered in Northern and Central Ontario.

1. **Kapuskasing** (Northern Ontario) — 88%
2. **Espanola** (Northern Ontario) — 86%
3. **Kenora** (Northern Ontario) — 84%
4. **Bancroft** (Central Ontario) — 83%
5. **North Bay** (Northern Ontario) — 83%
6. **Owen Sound** (Central Ontario) — 83%
7. **Timmins** (Northern Ontario) — 83%
8. **Huntsville** (Central Ontario) — 82%
9. **Winchester** (Eastern Ontario) — 81%
10. **Belleville** (Eastern Ontario) — 79%

Seven of the top ten are in Northern or Central Ontario. Four centres are tied at 83%, which shows just how consistent the smaller-centre advantage is at the top of the rankings. The highest-ranked GTA centre doesn't appear anywhere near this list.

## Top 10 centres by G2 pass rate

The G2 is the first road test most Ontario drivers take, usually after holding a G1 for at least 12 months (or 8 months with a certified driving course). The ranking shifts slightly from the G test, but the same regions dominate.

1. **Bancroft** (Central Ontario) — 86%
2. **Kapuskasing** (Northern Ontario) — 84%
3. **Kenora** (Northern Ontario) — 82%
4. **Espanola** (Northern Ontario) — 81%
5. **Owen Sound** (Central Ontario) — 81%
6. **Winchester** (Eastern Ontario) — 80%
7. **North Bay** (Northern Ontario) — 80%
8. **Timmins** (Northern Ontario) — 79%
9. **Walkerton** (Southwestern Ontario) — 79%
10. **Sault Ste Marie** (Northern Ontario) — 79%

Bancroft takes the top spot for the G2 with 86% — the highest figure of any centre for either test type. That is 28 percentage points higher than Brampton, which sits at the bottom of the province at 58%. Two equally prepared drivers could face very different outcomes depending only on where they choose to test.

## Why smaller centres score higher

The pattern in the data is consistent: smaller towns outperform larger urban centres across both test types. Several factors contribute to this gap.

Traffic volume is the most visible one. Centres in places like Kapuskasing, Espanola, and Kenora serve smaller populations with quieter roads. Test routes in these areas have fewer intersections, fewer pedestrians, and less unpredictable behaviour from other drivers. That means fewer high-pressure moments where a nervous test-taker might make a critical error. When you are not fighting through congestion, you can focus on demonstrating clean technique.

Route familiarity also plays a role. Smaller centres work with a limited set of test routes in a compact area. Local test-takers who practise those specific roads before test day can reduce the element of surprise. Knowing exactly where the school zones, tricky intersections, and speed-limit changes are located makes a real difference in performance.

Driving complexity matters too. High-volume urban centres process hundreds of tests per week in conditions that are inherently harder. Merging into dense traffic, navigating multi-lane intersections, and reacting to aggressive drivers or construction zones all raise the difficulty. A left turn at a busy Mississauga intersection is objectively harder to execute cleanly than one on a quiet street in Winchester or Walkerton.

This does not mean smaller centres give out free passes. The evaluation criteria are set by DriveTest and apply everywhere in Ontario. The driving environment at smaller locations is simply less demanding, which gives test-takers more room to show competence without the compounding stress of heavy urban traffic. ## Where GTA centres rank

If you are testing in the Greater Toronto Area, the numbers are hard to ignore. Every GTA centre falls in the bottom half of the provincial rankings for both test types. Here is how GTA centres compare on the G test:

- **Brampton** — 58%
- **Toronto Downsview** — 60%
- **Toronto Etobicoke** — 61%
- **Newmarket** — 61%
- **Toronto Metro East** — 63%
- **Mississauga** — 63%
- **Toronto Port Union** — 66%
- **Hamilton** — 66%
- **Burlington** — 69%
- **Oshawa** — 69%
- **Oakville** — 70%

The G2 results follow a similar pattern. Brampton and Toronto Downsview again anchor the bottom at 58% each for the G2 test.

The spread even within the GTA is worth noting. Oakville posts a 70% G test figure, which is twelve points higher than Brampton's 58%. If you live in the western GTA and have access to a car, the drive to Oakville or Burlington could be a real advantage over testing in Brampton or Etobicoke.

That said, location is only one variable. A test-taker who has practised extensively in downtown Toronto traffic may actually be better prepared for a GTA test route than someone who rarely drives in the city. The data shows averages across all test-takers at each centre, not a prediction for any individual driver. Use it as one input in your decision, not the only one.
